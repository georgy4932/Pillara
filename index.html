The challenge

Managing medicines
is harder than it should be.

Most people rely on memory, paper notes, or nothing at all. It’s easy to get things wrong — and the consequences can be serious.

01

Forgotten doses happen more when you’re juggling more than one medicine at once.

02

Medicine labels are written for professionals, not the patients who actually use them.

03

A painkiller or cold remedy from the pharmacy could interact with your current medicines without you knowing.

04

GP appointments come and go without the questions you meant to ask ever getting answered.

05

Managing medicines for someone else adds another layer of stress and complexity for carers and families.

What the app does

Four tools.
One beautiful app.

⏰

Smart medication reminders

Set a schedule for every medicine, get timely reminders on your lock screen, and track what you’ve taken — so staying consistent feels easier, not heavier.

📖

Clear medicine instructions

Every medicine in your list comes with simple, jargon-free guidance — when to take it, how to take it, what to watch out for, and why it matters.

📅

Appointment support

Track GP and pharmacist appointments, prepare your questions in advance, and follow up on action points — so each visit feels organised and useful.

🔍

OTC safety checker

Before buying a painkiller, supplement, or cold remedy, check it against your medicine record. Pillara flags anything that may need extra caution or a pharmacist conversation.

Questions & answers

Things people
want to know.

Is Pillara a replacement for my doctor or pharmacist?
No. Pillara is a support tool that helps you stay organised and informed between appointments. Always consult your GP or pharmacist for clinical decisions.
Which devices will the app work on?
Pillara is planned for iPhone, iPad, Android phones and tablets, and as a web app. Tell us your preferred platform when you sign up — it helps us prioritise what to build first.
Can it help if I take several medicines?
Yes. Pillara is being designed for people managing multiple medicines, with separate reminders and guidance for each part of the routine.
How does the OTC safety checker work?
You search or scan a product you’re considering — like a painkiller, antihistamine, or supplement — and Pillara checks it against your personal medicine record. If there is a concern, you see a clear alert and next-step guidance.
Is Pillara suitable for carers?
Yes. Pillara is being designed to support carers and families managing medicines on behalf of loved ones, not just individual patients.
Is my health information secure?
Pillara is being built with encryption, secure handling, and responsible data practices in mind. Full privacy details will be published before launch.
When will the app be available?
Pillara is currently in development. Join the waitlist to be notified when early access opens — and to help shape what gets built first.
Can pharmacies or GP practices get involved?
Yes. Healthcare organisations interested in pilots or partnerships are welcome to join the waitlist or contact us directly.
